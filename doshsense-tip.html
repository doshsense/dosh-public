The Real Price of Convenience

Convenience costs money - not just the obvious premiums like delivery fees and last-minute bookings, but the quieter ones too. Ready meals. The nearest parking spot. Subscriptions that save you ten minutes a week. None are wrong. But they add up faster than most people realise.

The question isn't whether convenience is worth paying for - sometimes it absolutely is. It's whether you're choosing it consciously or defaulting to it automatically. Paying a premium for inertia is the one worth cutting. Paying for genuine time or energy savings can be worth every dollar.
