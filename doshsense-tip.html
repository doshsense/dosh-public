Opportunity Cost - Every Decision Has a Hidden Price

Opportunity cost is what you give up by choosing one thing over another. $300 spent on a weekend away isn't just $300 - invested at 7% for 20 years, that's over $1,100. The decision might still be worth it. But knowing the true cost changes its quality.

This isn't about eliminating enjoyment. It's about choosing which experiences are worth the opportunity cost - and which ones aren't. That's the difference between intentional and default spending.

💡 Try It in Dosh
Look at this week's Lifestyle Flex in Dosh. Pick the biggest transaction and ask - if I'd invested that instead, what could it have become? Not to regret it. Just to make the opportunity cost visible before the next similar decision.
