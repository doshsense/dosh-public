Loss Aversion - Why Losing Hurts More Than Winning Feels Good

Losing $100 feels roughly twice as bad as gaining $100 feels good. This asymmetry drives a huge number of poor financial decisions - holding losing investments too long, avoiding necessary risks, staying in bad situations because the pain of change feels larger than the gain.

Before any decision driven by fear of loss, ask: am I avoiding this because it's genuinely risky, or because losing feels worse than it is? Asking the question puts you ahead of most people who act on the feeling without ever examining it.

Look at your Recurring Bills in DoshSense. Is there anything you're keeping out of fear of losing what you've already spent? A service you're not using, a subscription you're scared to cancel in case you need it later? That's loss aversion. Name it and decide deliberately.
