The Hidden Cost of a Bad Financial Decision Made Early

Bad financial decisions made early have an outsized impact because of time. The true cost isn't the money lost - it's the opportunity cost compounded over years of recovery. High-interest debt at 22 carried for five years doesn't just cost interest. It costs what that money could have become.

This isn't a reason for regret. It's a reason for urgency now. The best time to make a good financial decision was ten years ago. The second best time is today. The decisions you make this month matter more than they feel like they do.

💡 Try It in DoshSense
Open DoshSense and look at your Recurring Bills. Is there a high-interest debt in there? Calculate roughly what you're paying in interest per month. Then ask - what would that same amount become if it were going into savings instead? That gap is the cost of waiting to act.
