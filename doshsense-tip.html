Anchoring Bias - Why the First Number You See Changes Everything

A jacket marked down from $400 to $200 feels like a bargain - even if $200 is still more than you'd have paid without seeing the original price. The $400 anchor changed your perception of value without changing the actual value.

Retailers use anchoring deliberately. The defence is simple: before any significant purchase, ask what you would have paid before you saw any price at all. That number is your true anchor. Use it instead of theirs.

💡 Try It in DoshSense
Look at this week's Lifestyle Flex in DoshSense. For any purchase over $50 - did you buy it because of a sale or discount? If yes, ask honestly: would you have bought it at full price? If not, the anchor did its job. Now you know what to watch for next time.
