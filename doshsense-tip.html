Cashflow Timing - When Money Moves Matters as Much as How Much

Two people with identical incomes can have completely different stress levels based purely on timing. Income arriving on the 28th with rent due on the 1st creates three days of anxiety that income on the 1st eliminates. Timing is one of the most overlooked dimensions of personal finance.

Fixed commitments should leave immediately after payday before discretionary spending absorbs them. If your timing is misaligned, shifting direct debit dates is often a simple fix - and can reduce financial stress significantly without changing a single spending decision.

💡 Try It in DoshSense
Open your Recurring Bills in DoshSense and look at the dates. When does income arrive? When do the biggest bills leave? Is the sequence working in your favour - or against it? If bills are going out before income arrives, that timing gap is worth fixing this week.
